Loan eligibility age, income, employment type, and credit history batti vary avutundi. General criteria below — agent meeru exact profile check chestadu.

  • 🎂

    Age

  • 💰

    Minimum Income

  • 🏢

  • ⭐

    Banking History

    Active bank account with regular credit turnover — 6–12 months bank statement required

Factors Affecting Loan Eligibility

What determines your loan eligibility?

Multiple factors together decide how much loan you can get and at what interest rate. Understanding these helps you optimize before applying.

💰

Monthly Net Income

Higher the income, higher the eligible loan amount. Banks allow 40–50% of monthly income towards EMI — called FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio).

⭐

CIBIL / Credit Score

750+ score = best rates and maximum amount. Below 700 = higher interest or possible rejection. Score reflects repayment history on all past and current credit.

📊

Existing EMI Obligations

Existing home loan, car loan, personal loan EMIs reduce available FOIR for new loan. Higher existing EMIs = lower new loan eligibility amount.

🎂

Age & Loan Tenure

Younger age = longer maximum tenure available = lower EMI per lakh = higher eligible amount. Nearing retirement age = shorter tenure = higher EMI = lower eligibility.

🏢

Employment Type & Stability

Government employee > MNC employee > private company > self-employed in terms of risk. Stable employment history increases confidence and loan amount significantly.

🏡

Collateral / Property Value

Secured loans (home loan, LAP) depend on property value. Higher property value = higher loan amount possible. LTV (Loan to Value) ratio caps at 70–80%.
