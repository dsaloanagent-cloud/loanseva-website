Eligibility Criteria

Who can apply for a Plot & Construction Loan?

Salaried, self-employed, NRI — wide eligibility. Basic income and property conditions satisfy chesthe loan possible.

👔 Salaried Applicants

  • 🎂

    Age

    21 to 60 years at the time of application (up to 70 for self-employed)

  • 💰

    Minimum Income

    ₹25,000/month net salary (varies by bank and city)

  • 📅

    Employment

    Minimum 2 years total employment; 6 months in current job

  • 📊

    CIBIL Score

    700 or above preferred; 650+ considered with good profile

💡 Government employees get better rates and higher LTV. Central/state government, PSU, defence employees qualify for preferential schemes.

🏢 Self-Employed / NRI Applicants

  • 🎂

    Age

    25 to 70 years; business must be operational for 3+ years

  • 💰

    Minimum Income

    ₹3 Lakhs+ annual profit as per ITR (last 2 years)

  • 🏗️

    Construction Plan

    Approved building plan / municipal permission required for construction loan

  • 🌍

    NRI Eligibility

    Indian passport / OCI holders eligible; NRE/NRO account mandatory

💡 Plot within municipal limits gets better approval chances. DTCP / HMDA / VUDA approved layouts are strongly preferred by banks.

Required Documents

Keep these documents ready

Plot & construction loans need property documents along with income proof. Agent will guide you on what's needed step by step.

🆔 Identity & Address Proof

  • Aadhaar Card (mandatory)
  • PAN Card (mandatory)
  • Passport / Voter ID / Driving Licence
  • Utility bill for current address proof

💰 Income Documents

  • Last 3 months salary slips (salaried)
  • Last 6 months bank statement
  • Last 2 years ITR with computation
  • Form 16 / CA certificate (if applicable)

🏗️ Property Documents

  • Sale deed / Agreement to sell
  • EC (Encumbrance Certificate) — 13/30 years
  • Approved building plan (for construction)
  • Property tax receipts / Patta copy

📋 Additional Documents

  • Title deed chain documents (from original)
  • NOC from layout developer / society
  • Construction estimate from civil engineer
  • 2 recent passport-size photographs
