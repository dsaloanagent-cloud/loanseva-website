Eligibility Criteria

Who can buy a Term Insurance plan?

Salaried professionals, self-employed individuals, and homemakers can all apply with basic eligibility checks.

👤 Individual Eligibility

  • 🎂

    Age

    18 to 65 years at entry, coverage up to age 99 possible

  • 💼

    Occupation

    Salaried, self-employed, or business owners — most occupations covered

  • 💰

    Income Proof

    Required for higher sum assured (₹1 Crore+) to assess insurability

  • 🩺

    Medical Tests

    May be required based on age, sum assured, and health declaration

💡 Non-smokers get significantly lower premiums than smokers for the same coverage — agent will guide on the best plan for your profile.

📋 Documentation Basics

  • 🆔

    KYC Compliance

    Valid Aadhaar and PAN required as per regulatory norms

  • 🏥

    Health Declaration

    Honest disclosure of existing health conditions for valid claims

  • 👨‍👩‍👧

    Nominee Details

    Nominee (usually family member) must be declared at policy start

  • 📊

    Existing Policies

    Disclosure of other life insurance policies held, if any

💡 Pre-existing conditions don't automatically disqualify you — many insurers offer cover with a premium loading or specific exclusions. Agent will find the right insurer.

Required Documents

Keep these documents ready

Term insurance requires standard KYC, income, and health-related documentation. We help collect everything digitally.

🆔 Identity & Address Proof

  • Aadhaar Card (mandatory)
  • PAN Card (mandatory)
  • Passport / Voter ID / Driving Licence
  • Recent utility bill for address proof

💰 Income Documents

  • Last 3 months salary slips (salaried)
  • Last 2–3 years ITR (self-employed)
  • Bank statement (last 6 months)
  • Form 16 (if applicable)

🩺 Medical & Health Documents

  • Health declaration form
  • Medical test reports (if required by insurer)
  • Existing illness / treatment records (if any)
  • Family medical history declaration

📸 Photographs & Others

  • 1–2 recent passport-size photographs
  • Signed proposal form
  • Nominee identity & relationship proof
  • Existing policy details (if any)

📱 Most documents can be submitted digitally via WhatsApp — medical tests, if needed, can be scheduled at your home.

Available Coverage Options

Which term insurance variants can you choose?

Different term plan structures suit different needs and budgets — agent helps you pick the right one.

📄

Pure Term Plan

Simple life cover with lowest premium — no maturity benefit, pure protection for your family.

💰

Return of Premium (ROP)

Get all premiums paid back at the end of the policy term if you outlive it, at a slightly higher premium.

📈

Increasing Cover Plan

Sum assured increases periodically to keep pace with inflation and rising responsibilities.

🏥

Term Plan with Critical Illness Rider

Add-on coverage that pays out on diagnosis of specified critical illnesses, in addition to life cover.

🚑

Term Plan with Accidental Death Rider

Additional payout in case of death due to accident, over and above the base sum assured.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Joint Life / Family Term Plan

Covers both spouses or family members under a single policy with combined or individual sum assured options.

Premium, Coverage & Policy Details

What can you expect on your Term Plan?

Indicative figures based on age and health profile — agent gives you exact quotes after a quick eligibility check.

👤 Age 25–30, Non-Smoker

  • ₹1 Crore cover starting ~₹600–800/month
  • Policy term: up to 35–40 years
  • Minimal medical requirements
  • Lowest premium bracket overall

👤 Age 35–40, Non-Smoker

  • ₹1 Crore cover starting ~₹1,000–1,500/month
  • Policy term: up to 25–30 years
  • Basic medical tests usually required
  • Moderate premium bracket

👤 Age 45+, Non-Smoker

  • ₹1 Crore cover starting ~₹2,500–4,000/month
  • Policy term: up to age 75–80
  • Detailed medical tests required
  • Higher premium due to age & risk factors

📊 Policy Charges & Notes

  • Smokers pay 30–50% higher premium typically
  • GST applicable on premium as per current rates
  • Free-look period of 15–30 days post issuance
  • Grace period for premium payment (varies by insurer)
