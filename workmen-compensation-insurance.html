About This Policy

What is Workmen Compensation Insurance?

A mandatory business insurance policy that protects employers from financial liability when an employee suffers a work-related injury, disability, or death — and ensures workers receive rightful compensation without delay.

⚖️

Legal Mandate — WC Act 1923

The Workmen Compensation Act, 1923 (now Employees' Compensation Act) makes it mandatory for certain categories of employers to compensate workers. Non-compliance attracts heavy penalties and court liability.

🏗️

Who Needs It Most

Construction companies, factories, warehouses, farms, transport operators, and any business employing manual or skilled labour at worksite — WC policy is not optional for these sectors.

👷

Covers All Workers

Permanent employees, contract labourers, casual workers, and daily wage workers — all are entitled to compensation under the Act. WC policy ensures the insurer pays, not the employer directly.

🛡️

Employer Financial Protection

Without WC policy, a single fatal accident claim can cost ₹5–15 Lakhs or more out of pocket. The policy transfers this liability to the insurer, protecting business cash flow and assets.

Eligibility Criteria

Who should get Workmen Compensation Insurance?

Any business employing workers — especially in physical, manual, or hazardous roles — needs WC insurance for legal compliance and employee protection.

🏢 Businesses That Must Have WC Policy

  • 🏗️

    Construction & Infrastructure Companies

    Building contractors, civil engineers, infrastructure developers — highest-risk category. WC policy mandatory and also required for government tender submissions.

  • 🏭

    Factories & Manufacturing Units

    Any factory under the Factories Act with workmen engaged in machinery operation, assembly, or production — WC policy is a legal requirement.

  • 🚛

    Transport & Logistics Operators

    Drivers, loaders, cleaners employed in goods transport — their road-related injury risk is high and WC policy is mandatory under Motor Vehicles Act too.

  • 🌾

    Agriculture & Farm Labour Employers

    Plantation workers, agricultural labourers employed on a regular or seasonal basis — covered under the WC Act and require policy backing.

💡 Contractors & Sub-contractors — Even if you are a sub-contractor, WC policy mandatory unte principal employer liability lo meeru kooda padadam avoid cheyyadaniki policy teesukōvali. Agent contractor-specific structure help chestadu.

👔 Who Can Buy WC Insurance

  • 🏪

    SMEs & Micro Businesses

    Even small shops, retail units, and small workshops with 1–10 workers can and should get WC policy — premium is very affordable starting at ₹3,000/year.

  • 🏢

    Corporates & Large Employers

    Large corporates with 500+ employees need group WC policies with proper wage schedules, risk classification, and annual renewal process.

  • 🤝

    Labour Contractors

    Contractors who supply labour to principal employers — WC policy in contractor's name protects both the contractor and the principal employer from liability.

  • 🏘️

    Housing Societies & Real Estate Developers

    Apartment construction, civil maintenance work, security staff — WC policy needed for any workmen deployed on property for construction or maintenance.

💡 Domestic employers — House construction lo daily wage workers employ chesthe kuda WC liability apply avutundi. Agent correct policy structure suggest chestadu.

Employee Coverage & Protection

What does your WC policy cover?

Workmen Compensation Insurance covers a broad range of work-related injuries and events — protecting both workers and employers comprehensively.

💀

Accidental Death at Workplace

Fatal accidents during course of employment — compensation paid to legal heirs based on worker's age and wage as per WC Act schedule.

🦽

Permanent Total Disability

Worker permanently unable to work due to workplace accident — full compensation equivalent to death benefit paid to the injured worker.

🩹

Permanent Partial Disability

Loss of limb, eyesight, or partial loss of body function — compensation proportional to percentage of disability as listed in WC Act Schedule I.

🏥

Medical Treatment Expenses

Hospitalisation, surgery, medication, and rehabilitation costs arising from a work-related injury — covered up to the policy's specified limit.

⏱️

Temporary Partial Disablement

Worker unable to work for a period due to injury — weekly compensation of 25% of wages paid during temporary incapacity up to 5 years.

🦠

Occupational Diseases

Diseases contracted due to the nature of work — e.g. silicosis in mining, lung disease in chemical plants — covered under the extended WC policy.

Premium, Coverage & Claim Process

What to expect with your WC policy?

Indicative premium figures and claim process — agent gives exact quotes after reviewing your workforce details and risk profile.

💵 Indicative Premiums

  • Clerical / office workers: ₹0.5–1% of wage bill
  • Skilled trade workers: ₹1–2% of wage bill
  • Construction labourers: ₹2–3.5% of wage bill
  • Mining / high-risk: ₹3–5% of wage bill

🧮 Compensation Formula

  • Death: 50% of monthly wages × relevant factor
  • PTD: 60% of monthly wages × relevant factor
  • PPD: % of PTD compensation based on schedule
  • Minimum compensation: ₹1,20,000 (death/PTD)

📋 How to Raise a Claim

  • Inform agent and insurer within 48 hours of accident
  • File FIR (if fatal) and get medical report
  • Submit claim form with all supporting documents
  • Insurer appoints surveyor — settlement in 30–60 days

🚫 Common Exclusions

  • Injury under influence of drugs or alcohol
  • Self-inflicted injury or wilful disobedience
  • Non-work-related accidents outside duty scope
  • War, nuclear risk, and civil commotion
