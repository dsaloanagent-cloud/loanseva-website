Eligibility Criteria

Who can apply for a Kisan Credit Card?

Owner cultivators, tenant farmers, and group members can all apply with proper land/cultivation proof.

🌾 Individual Farmers

  • 🎂

    Age

    18 to 75 years (co-applicant needed above 60 in some banks)

  • 📜

    Land Ownership

    Owner cultivators with clear land title / pattadar passbook

  • 🌱

    Cultivation Proof

    Active cultivation of crops, horticulture, or allied activities

  • 📊

    Credit History

    No major default on existing agricultural loans

💡 Tenant farmers & sharecroppers without land title can also apply through JLG (Joint Liability Group) — agent will guide on the right structure.

👥 Groups & Allied Activities

  • 🤝

    Joint Liability Groups (JLG)

    Group of farmers cultivating jointly or individually as JLG members

  • 🐄

    Allied Activity Farmers

    Dairy, poultry, fisheries, and animal husbandry practitioners eligible

  • 🌾

    SHG Members

    Self-Help Group members engaged in agriculture also covered

  • 📋

    Co-Applicants

    Joint pattadars / co-owners can apply together for higher limits

💡 Fisheries & animal husbandry farmers get a separate KCC-AH&F variant with similar benefits — agent will confirm which scheme fits you.

Required Documents

Keep these documents ready

KCC requires land and cultivation proof along with standard KYC. We help collect and verify everything.

🆔 Identity & Address Proof

  • Aadhaar Card (mandatory)
  • PAN Card (mandatory for higher limits)
  • Voter ID / Driving Licence
  • Recent address proof if different from land records

📜 Land & Cultivation Documents

  • Pattadar passbook / Land title deed
  • Adangal / Revenue records (1B / RoR)
  • Tenant cultivation agreement (if applicable)
  • No-dues certificate from other banks

🌱 Crop & Income Details

  • Crop pattern / cultivation declaration
  • Existing loan statements (if any)
  • Bank account passbook / statement
  • Allied activity proof (dairy/poultry, if applicable)

📸 Photographs & Others

  • 2 recent passport-size photographs
  • Signed application form
  • Co-applicant documents (joint pattadars)
  • JLG / SHG membership proof (if applicable)

📱 Documents can be shared digitally via WhatsApp — agent helps coordinate with village revenue officials if needed.

Eligible Farmers & Activities

Which farmers and activities qualify?

KCC covers crop production as well as several allied agricultural activities.

🌾

Crop Cultivation

Paddy, cotton, chillies, maize, and other seasonal & cash crops grown by owner or tenant farmers.

🥭

Horticulture & Plantation

Fruit orchards, vegetable farming, and plantation crops like mango, banana, and coconut.

🐄

Dairy & Animal Husbandry

Dairy farming, livestock rearing, and cattle-related working capital needs under KCC-AH&F.

🐟

Fisheries & Aquaculture

Fish farming, prawn cultivation, and inland/coastal aquaculture activities are covered.

🐓

Poultry Farming

Small and medium poultry units for working capital like feed, chicks, and medicines.

👥

Tenant Farmers & JLGs

Farmers without land title can access KCC through Joint Liability Groups with group guarantee.

Benefits

Why a Kisan Credit Card is the right choice

KCC is designed specifically to support farmers with affordable, flexible, and timely agricultural credit.

📉 Lowest Effective Interest Rate

With government interest subvention, effective rate can be as low as 4% p.a. on timely repayment of crop loans.

🔓 Collateral-Free Limits

No security needed for credit limits up to ₹1.6–2 Lakhs, making it accessible for small and marginal farmers.

🔄 Revolving Credit Facility

Withdraw and repay multiple times within your sanctioned limit — pay interest only on amount used.

🌱 Covers Crop + Allied Needs

Single card covers crop production, post-harvest expenses, and allied activities like dairy or fisheries.

🛡️ Built-In Crop Insurance

KCC holders can be covered under PMFBY crop insurance, protecting against crop failure due to natural calamities.

💳 Easy Access via ATM/RuPay Card

Withdraw funds easily through ATM or RuPay KCC debit card — no need to visit branch every time.

Credit Limit, Repayment & Rates

What can you expect on your KCC?

Indicative figures based on land holding and crop pattern — agent gives you exact figures after eligibility check.

🌾 Crop Loan Component

  • Limit based on scale of finance per acre
  • Rate: 7% p.a. (4% effective with subvention)
  • Repayment: linked to crop harvest cycle
  • Amount: ₹50,000 – ₹3 Lakhs (small/marginal farmers)

🐄 Allied Activity Component

  • Additional limit for dairy/poultry/fisheries
  • Rate: 7% – 9% p.a.
  • Repayment: as per activity cash flow cycle
  • Amount: assessed separately based on activity scale

🏠 Consumption / Term Component

  • For post-harvest & household needs
  • Rate: as applicable for crop component
  • Repayment: 5 years, with annual review
  • Amount: 10% of crop loan limit typically

📊 Processing & Renewal

  • Processing fee: nil or minimal up to ₹3 Lakhs
  • Card valid 5 years with annual review
  • No prepayment penalty on crop loans
  • Renewal requires updated land & crop records
Government Subsidies & Support

How the government supports KCC holders

Multiple central and state schemes work alongside KCC to reduce your effective cost of borrowing.

💰 Interest Subvention Scheme

Central government provides 2% interest subvention on crop loans up to ₹3 Lakhs, bringing effective rate down significantly.

Up to 2% subvention

⭐ Prompt Repayment Incentive (PRI)

Additional 3% interest incentive for farmers who repay their crop loan on time — bringing effective rate to as low as 4% p.a.

3% extra on time payment

🛡️ PMFBY Crop Insurance

KCC holders can opt into Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana for protection against crop loss due to natural calamities.

Crop loss protection

🏛️ State Government Top-Up Schemes

AP & Telangana state governments periodically announce additional interest waivers or loan relief schemes for farmers.

State-specific benefits

📋 Subsidy terms change periodically based on government notifications. Agent keeps you updated on the latest applicable benefits at the time of application.
