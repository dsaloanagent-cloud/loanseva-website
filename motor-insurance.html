Eligibility Criteria

Who can buy Motor Insurance?

Any registered vehicle owner in India can buy motor insurance. Eligibility is simple — agent confirms details based on your vehicle type and registration.

🚗 Car Insurance Eligibility

  • 📋

    Vehicle Registration

    Valid RC (Registration Certificate) under owner's name — AP, TS, or any state registration accepted

  • 🪪

    Driving Licence

    Valid DL of the vehicle owner or primary driver — required at time of claim

  • 📅

    Vehicle Age

    New cars to cars up to 15–20 years old — older vehicles need inspection; premium varies with age

  • 🏷️

    Previous Policy

    Existing policy copy needed for renewal — claim history affects No Claim Bonus (NCB)

💡 New car buyers — dealer bundled insurance compare cheyyi. Memu often 20–30% takkuva premium tō same or better coverage find chestāmu — agent check chestādu.

🏍️ Bike Insurance Eligibility

  • 📋

    RC Book

    Two-wheeler registration certificate — bike engine & chassis number required for policy issuance

  • 🪪

    Valid Driving Licence

    Two-wheeler DL — learner's licence not sufficient for claim; proper DL mandatory

  • 📅

    Bike Age & CC

    Premium varies based on engine cubic capacity (CC) and manufacturing year — agent calculates exactly

  • 🏆

    No Claim Bonus (NCB)

    5% to 50% NCB discount for claim-free years — transferable when switching insurers

💡 Bike insurance is compulsory by Motor Vehicles Act — riding without valid insurance can result in fine up to ₹2,000 and/or imprisonment. Don't let policy lapse!

Car & Bike Insurance Coverage Options

Choose the right coverage for your vehicle

Third-party mandatory, comprehensive recommended, and add-ons for complete protection — agent helps pick the exact right combination.

⚖️

Third-Party Insurance

Legally mandatory. Covers damage to third-party vehicle, property, or person. Does not cover your own vehicle damage. Lowest premium.

🛡️

Comprehensive Insurance

Own damage + third-party both covered. Accidents, theft, fire, flood, natural calamities — wide coverage. Recommended for all vehicles.

🔩

Zero Depreciation (Bumper-to-Bumper)

Claim without depreciation deduction on plastic, rubber, glass parts. Best for new cars and premium bikes. Full part replacement value.

🔑

Engine Protection Add-On

Covers engine damage due to water ingression / hydrostatic lock — critical in flood-prone areas of AP & Telangana like Hyderabad.

🚨

Roadside Assistance (RSA)

24×7 breakdown support — towing, flat tyre change, fuel delivery, battery jump-start anywhere in India. Minimal add-on cost.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Passenger / Pillion Cover

Personal accident cover for passengers in car or pillion rider on bike. Medical expenses and disability covered. Often overlooked add-on.

Key Terms & IRDAI Regulations

Important things every vehicle owner must know

IRDAI regulations, premium structures, and important motor insurance terms — agent explains all of these clearly before policy purchase.

📊 IDV — Insured Declared Value

IDV is the maximum claim amount for total loss or theft — approximate current market value of the vehicle. Higher IDV = higher premium but better claim payout. Agent helps set optimal IDV.

Choose IDV carefully

🏆 NCB — No Claim Bonus

For every claim-free policy year, you earn 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, or 50% discount on own damage premium. NCB is transferable when switching insurers — never lose your earned discount.

Up to 50% premium saving

⚖️ Third-Party — IRDAI Fixed Premiums

Third-party insurance premiums are set by IRDAI every year — not negotiable. Own damage (OD) premiums vary by insurer. Agent compares OD premium + add-ons to find best deal.

Legally mandated coverage

🔩 Zero Dep — Full Part Value

Standard comprehensive policies deduct depreciation on plastic, rubber, glass parts during claims. Zero Depreciation add-on removes this deduction — you get full part replacement value always.

Recommended for new vehicles

📋 Motor insurance terms and premium rates change annually per IRDAI circulars. Agent keeps you updated on the latest applicable rates and coverage rules at the time of purchase.
